The New York City calendar

What is coming for the city — the days it will feel, the deadlines that bind it, the anniversaries it is about to mark. Sized by how much they matter, not by who filed them.

How this works. Five sources are merged every twelve hours and scored 0-100 for public significance, which decides how big each event appears. Round-number anniversaries are computed fresh on every run from a corpus of 282 sourced events in the city's history, so the calendar re-earns itself each year. Hearings are capped at 62 by design: the most consequential committee hearing of the year can be notable, but it will never outrank an election. Read the full methodology →

This calendar was built with AI assistance. The hearings, agendas and session days come directly from government feeds, and the historical events are drawn from a sourced corpus. But the significance scores are editorial judgments expressed as numbers, and any step can carry an error. The dates of curated events marked approximate are not yet confirmed by the responsible agency. Check the methodology before relying on any of it.
